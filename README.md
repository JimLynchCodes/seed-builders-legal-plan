🧠 1. Clarify Mission & Vision

Before a single form is filed:

- Define your mission statement (clear, concise, nonprofit purpose).

- Ensure it qualifies under IRS 501(c)(3) standards: charitable, educational, religious, scientific, etc.

- Decide your programs/services — how will you actually serve your community?

<br/>

🧾 2. Choose a Legal Structure

In New York, most 501(c)(3)s are set up as:

- Not-for-Profit Corporations (NY State)

  - Offers liability protection.

  - Required for IRS recognition.

You cannot operate as an unregistered nonprofit and claim tax-exempt donations.

<br/>

📍 3. Register with New York State

A. Choose a Name

- Must be unique in NY.

- Must include a nonprofit indicator (“Inc.” or similar).

B. Draft and File the Articles of Incorporation
File with:

- New York Department of State

  - Must include specific IRS-required language (purpose & dissolution clause).

  - You’ll pay a state filing fee.

C. Appoint Initial Directors
New York requires at least three directors.

D. Create Bylaws

- Governing document — internal rules.

- Not filed with the state but required for IRS.

E. Hold Organizational Meeting

- Adopt bylaws

- Elect officers

<br/>

💳 4. Obtain Federal Employer Identification (EIN)

- Apply online with the IRS

- Free, required even if you don’t hire employees

- Needed for banking, tax forms, grants, etc.

🏢 5. Register with New York Attorney General’s Charities Bureau

- Because you’ll be soliciting donations:

- Register before fundraising.

- Periodic reporting required.

- There’s a separate set of forms and renewals.

📑 6. Apply for 501(c)(3) Tax-Exempt Status

File IRS Form 1023 (or 1023-EZ if eligible)

- Includes:

  - Narrative of activities

  - Budget projections

  - Governance info

- Expect waiting period of several months to a year.

- IRS recognition gives donors tax-deductible status.

<br/>

💰 7. Sales Tax / Reseller / Tax Exemption

Since you want to buy wholesale using a valid reseller certificate:

A. NY State Sales Tax Certificate of Authority

- You need a NY Sales Tax Certificate of Authority to purchase tax-exempt for resale.

- Apply through New York State Department of Taxation and Finance.

- Use this to buy materials/supplies for resale or fundraising.

B. Additional Sales Tax Exemption

- As a 501(c)(3), eligibility for NYS sales tax exemption on purchases used for your mission.

- There are specific NYS forms for nonprofits; make sure to understand what qualifies.

<br/>

🏦 8. Banking & Financial Systems

- Open a nonprofit bank account under your EIN.

- Implement internal controls (two signers, no personal accounts, etc.)

- Chart of accounts tailored for nonprofits.

<br/>

🧾 9. Accounting & Compliance

Nonprofits have special reporting:

- IRS Form 990 Series (990-N, 990-EZ, or 990) annually

- NY State Charities Bureau financial reports

- Sales tax filings (if you have a Certificate of Authority)

- Payroll filings (if you have employees or contractors)

Consider:

- QuickBooks or nonprofit accounting software

- Outsourced accountant familiar with nonprofits

<br/>

🧑‍🤝‍🧑 10. Fundraising & Donation Infrastructure

To accept tax-deductible donations:

- Set up online donation platforms (GiveLively, PayPal Nonprofit, etc.)

- Offer receipts that include:

  - Tax-exempt number

  - Donation amount

  - Statement of tax-deductibility

- Track donors for acknowledgments and compliance

<br/>

📣 11. Branding, Website & Marketing

Put the mission out there:

- Website with:

  - Mission

  - Programs

  - Donate button

  - IRS Determination Letter

- Social media

- Email newsletter

- Fundraising materials

<br/>

🧑‍⚖️ 12. Policies & Board Governance

Adopt core policies:

- Conflict of Interest Policy (required for IRS)

- Document retention

- Whistleblower

- Volunteer background

- Gift acceptance

- Compensation policy

<br/>

🧑‍🎓 13. Insurance

Nonprofits still have risk:

- General liability

- Directors & Officers (D&O) insurance

- Property

- Workers’ comp (if you have employees)

<br/>

📊 14. Ongoing Obligations

Annual requirements:

- IRS Form 990

- NY Charities Bureau annual report

- State corporate franchise reports (if any)

- Sales tax renewals

- Board meetings & minutes

<br/>

🧩 Extra Considerations

- Grant readiness — develop strong metrics and reporting systems

- 501(c)(3) fiscal sponsorship — if you want to operate before IRS status approved

- State vs Federal tax exemption are separate — ensure you apply both

<br/>

🗂️ High-Level Timeline
Stage	Typical Duration
Incorporation & EIN	1–4 weeks
NY State Charity Registration	2–8 weeks
IRS 501(c)(3) Approval	4–12+ months
Sales Tax Cert of Authority	2–6 weeks
Banking & Launch	1 week

<br/>

📌 Common Pitfalls to Avoid

❌ Filing IRS Form 1023 without proper bylaws or conflict policy
❌ Using personal accounts for nonprofit finances
❌ Waiting until after fundraising begins to register with NY Charities Bureau
❌ Misusing the reseller certificate (it’s not a blanket tax exemption)

<br/>

Good luck!
